# SUNDAY (dayIndex 0) — CONTEMPLATION / REVIEW
## Retrospective Meditation | ☉ Sun

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "To turn one's gaze inwards from time to time, even if only for five minutes daily. In so doing one should sink down into oneself, carefully take counsel with oneself, test and form one's principles of life."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "One attains a certain ability in such self-observation if one begins with the visualization of detached portions of daily life. One then becomes increasingly clever and skillful in such retrospect."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

3. "The student must see himself in a picture within his experiences — he must observe himself in his daily life as though from outside."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

4. "Labour to discover the essential, the enduring, and earnestly aim at goals in accord with it — not to fall into the mistake of thinking one has done something well, but to strive ever further toward the highest standards."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Pause now. If you were watching your morning from outside — as a stranger observing another person — what would you see? What pattern is repeating that you have not yet named?"

2. "Consider: what have you done today out of habit, and what out of genuine intention? Can you distinguish the two — right now, honestly?"

3. "If this day were the last page of a chapter in your biography, what would the observer notice that you have missed?"

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Tonight, reverse the day. Begin with this moment and move backward to waking. Do not judge — only observe. Where were you most awake? Where did consciousness slip away entirely?"

2. "In your backward review: find the moment where you acted most like yourself — and the moment you acted most like a machine. Hold both images without commentary."

3. "Review the day as pictures, not as thoughts. Let each scene appear and dissolve. Notice which scene carries the strongest feeling — that is where today's teaching hides."

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "This looking at one's experiences backward has a special value for spiritual training for the reason that it brings the soul to a point where it is able to release itself in thinking from the previous habit of merely following in thought the course of everyday events. In thought-retrospect one visualizes correctly, but one is not held to the sensory course of events. One needs this exercise to familiarize oneself with the spiritual world."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

2. text: "To turn one's gaze inwards from time to time — in so doing one should sink down into oneself, carefully take counsel with oneself, test and form one's principles of life, run through in thought one's knowledge — or lack of it — weigh up one's duties, think over the contents and true purpose of life, feel genuinely pained by one's own errors and imperfections."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands (Right Examination)"

3. text: "Whoever, in this way, endeavors to regulate his soul-life will also attain the possibility of self-observation through which he observes his own affairs with the same tranquillity as if they were those of others. The ability to behold one's own experiences, one's own joys and sorrows as though they were the joys and sorrows of others is a good preparation for spiritual training."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

4. text: "It is just by looking upon this ideal that he will create for himself the soul basis for the surrender of himself to meditations on symbolic and other thoughts and feelings. More and more it will become the ideal for the student to relate himself to life events in such a way that, with inner certainty and soul tranquillity, he allows them to approach him and does not judge them according to his soul condition, but according to their inner significance and their inner value."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

# MONDAY (dayIndex 1) — CONTROL OF THOUGHT
## Directed Thinking | ☽ Moon

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "The pupil must become the ruler in his world of thought. He is not the ruler if external circumstances, occupation, some tradition or other, social relationships, or the daily round of life determine a thought and how he works it out."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "Thoughts that dart to and fro like will-o'-the-wisps and follow each other in no logical or rational sequence, but merely by pure chance, destroy the form of the inner organ. The closer thought is made to follow upon thought, the more suitable will the form this sense organ develops."
   — Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V

3. "It is better if this exercise in thought-control is undertaken with a pin rather than with Napoleon. Thinking is then impelled to act out of its own energy — the essential thing here — whereas an interesting thought carries the thinking along with it."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

4. "If for a certain length of time we fasten our thoughts upon an object that is well known to us, we can be sure that we think in conformity with facts. Through simple thought exercises we acquire greater ability for factual thinking than through complicated and learned ideas."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Where today has your thinking wandered without your permission? Can you name the moment you lost the thread?"

2. "Right now — what thought were you holding before you saw this? Was it yours, or did something else place it there?"

3. "In the last hour, did a single thought arise that you chose deliberately — or has the stream carried you without your noticing?"

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "In your backward review tonight: find the moment your thinking was most truly your own — and the moment it was least. Hold both without judgment."

2. "Review one conversation from today. Where did your thinking follow the other person's logic, and where did it follow your own? Where did it follow neither — just noise?"

3. "Locate one thought today that you held for more than thirty seconds by your own will. If you cannot find one, that is the teaching."

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "The first condition is the cultivation of absolutely clear thinking. For this purpose a man must rid himself of the will-o'-the-wisps of thought, even if only for a very short time during the day — about five minutes. He must become the ruler in his world of thought. He is not the ruler if external circumstances, occupation, some tradition or other, the daily round of life, certain activities and so forth, determine a thought and how he works it out."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

2. text: "Therefore during this brief time, acting entirely out of his own free will, he must empty the soul of the ordinary, everyday course of thoughts and by his own initiative place one single thought at the centre of his soul. The thought need not be a particularly striking or interesting one. Indeed it will be all the better for what has to be attained in an occult respect if a thoroughly uninteresting and insignificant thought is chosen."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

3. text: "What is necessary for the thinking of man in spiritual training is, above all, objectivity. In the physical-sensory world, life is the human ego's great teacher of objectivity. If the human being turns his attention away from the physical-sensory world, he lacks the compulsory correction of the latter. If his thinking is then unable to be its own corrective, it must become irrational."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

4. text: "He singles out a particular thought and endeavors to link with it only such other thoughts as he can himself consciously and voluntarily produce. He rejects all casual ideas and does not connect this thought with another until he has investigated the origin of the latter."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V"

# TUESDAY (dayIndex 2) — CONTROL OF WILL
## Intentional Action | ♂ Mars

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "We try to think of some action which in the ordinary course of life we should certainly not have performed. Then we make it a duty to perform this action every day."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "The soul must become a ruler in the sphere of the will as it must be in the world of thought. In the physical-sensory world, it is life itself that appears as the ruler. In higher training man must become accustomed to obey his own commands strictly."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

3. "All inconstancy, all disharmony of action, is baneful for the lotus here in question. When the student performs some action he must see to it that his succeeding action follows in logical sequence."
   — Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V

4. "It is a good practice if one gives oneself for months, at a certain time of the day, the following command: Today, at this definite time, I shall perform this or that action. One then gradually becomes able to determine the time and the nature of the thing to be done so as to permit its being carried out with great exactness."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Have you performed your chosen action today — at the time you appointed? If not, what intercepted your will between intention and deed?"

2. "Name one thing you did this morning out of genuine resolve, and one thing you did because the situation pushed you. Which felt more like you?"

3. "Between waking and now — how many of your actions arose from your own initiative, and how many from reaction? Be precise."

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "In your backward review: find the moment where your will was most sovereign — most truly yours. Find also the moment it was most borrowed. What stood between them?"

2. "Review one deed from today. Trace it backward: was the impulse born in your I, or in your comfort? Were you the ruler or the ruled?"

3. "Did you carry out the small, self-chosen task at its appointed time? If yes — notice how it felt. If no — do not condemn, but observe what overpowered you. Name it."

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "We try to think of some action which in the ordinary course of life we should certainly not have performed. Then we make it a duty to perform this action every day. It will therefore be good to choose an action which can be performed every day and will occupy as long a period of time as possible. Again it is better to begin with some insignificant action which we have to force ourselves to perform; for example, to water at a fixed time every day a flower we have bought."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

2. text: "The soul must become a ruler in the sphere of the will as it must be in the world of thought. He who becomes accustomed to this will be less and less inclined to desire the non-essential. Dissatisfaction and instability in the life of will rest upon the desire for things the realization of which we cannot conceive clearly."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

3. text: "All inconstancy, all disharmony of action, is baneful for the lotus here in question. When the student performs some action he must see to it that his succeeding action follows in logical sequence, for if he acts from day to day with variable intent he will never develop the faculty here considered."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V"

4. text: "Every resolution is a force, and if this force does not produce an immediate effect, it works nevertheless on in its own way. All actions arising from desire are worthless in relation to the higher worlds. There, love for an action is alone the decisive factor."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter I"

# WEDNESDAY (dayIndex 3) — EQUANIMITY
## Emotional Balance | ☿ Mercury

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "In the third month, life should be centered on the development of a certain equanimity towards the fluctuations of joy and sorrow, pleasure and pain. 'Heights of jubilation' and 'depths of despair' should quite consciously be replaced by an equable mood."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "It is not the justified pain that one should suppress, but involuntary weeping; not the horror of an evil action, but the blind rage of anger; not attention to danger, but fruitless fear."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

3. "If we become angered, vexed or annoyed, we erect a wall around ourselves in the soul-world, and the forces which are to develop the eyes of the soul cannot approach."
   — Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter III

4. "With the joyful the soul should rejoice, with sadness it should feel pain. But it should acquire the ability to control the expression of joy and sorrow, of pleasure and pain."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Since waking, has any emotion carried you away — even briefly — past the point where your I remained present? What triggered it?"

2. "Observe your inner weather right now. Is there turbulence, or stillness? If turbulence — can you name its source without feeding it?"

3. "Recall the strongest feeling of this morning. Did you experience it, or did it experience you? There is a difference. Which was it?"

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "In your backward review: find the moment of strongest emotion today. Now hold that scene without the feeling. What remains when the emotion is removed — what is the fact underneath?"

2. "Review one moment of irritation or pleasure from today. Watch it as a weather pattern passing over a mountain. The mountain does not move. Were you the mountain, or the weather?"

3. "Tonight, notice: did equanimity visit you at any point today — even for a moment? If so, how did the world appear from that stillness? Hold that image."

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "In the third month, life should be centered on a new exercise — the development of a certain equanimity towards the fluctuations of joy and sorrow, pleasure and pain. Care is taken that no pleasure shall carry us away, no sorrow plunge us into the depths, no experience lead to immoderate anger or vexation, no expectation give rise to anxiety or fear, no situation disconcert us."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

2. text: "There need be no fear that such an exercise will make life arid and unproductive; far rather will it quickly be noticed that the experiences to which this exercise is applied are replaced by purer qualities of soul. Above all, if subtle attentiveness is maintained, an inner tranquillity in the body will one day become noticeable."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

3. text: "If we become angered, vexed or annoyed, we erect a wall around ourselves in the soul-world, and the forces which are to develop the eyes of the soul cannot approach. For instance, if a person angers me he sends forth a psychic current into the soul-world. I cannot see this current as long as I am myself capable of anger. My own anger conceals it from me."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter III"

4. text: "It is just in regard to the acquiring of this ability that much prejudice may result. One might imagine that one would become dull and without sympathy in regard to one's fellowmen. Yet the point is not this. One does not become less sensitive, but on the contrary more receptive to all that is joyous and sorrowful in one's environment than one was previously."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

# THURSDAY (dayIndex 4) — POSITIVITY
## Finding the Good | ♃ Jupiter

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "So must the esoteric pupil strive to seek for the positive in every phenomenon and in every being. He will soon notice that under the veil of something repugnant there is a hidden beauty, that even under the outer guise of a criminal there is a hidden good."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "The erroneous, the bad, the ugly should not prevent the soul from finding the true, the good, and the beautiful wherever it is present. This positiveness should not be confused with non-criticism."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

3. "Even as the sun does not withdraw its light from the bad and the evil, so the student, too, does not refuse them an intelligent sympathy."
   — Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V

4. "If you admire the beautiful teeth of a dead animal, you also see the decaying corpse. But this corpse does not prevent your seeing the beautiful teeth. One cannot consider the bad good and the false true, but it is possible not to be deterred by evil from seeing good."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Name one thing today that irritated or repelled you. Now — without pretending it was pleasant — what seed of development is hidden within it? Look again."

2. "Think of a person you encountered today. Before your opinion formed — what was true about them that your judgment almost obscured?"

3. "Where today did something imperfect cross your path? Can you perceive what it is trying to become — not what it fails to be?"

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "In your backward review: find one moment where you judged quickly. Now return to that scene and look for what you missed — the praiseworthy, the beautiful, the hidden potential."

2. "Review the day through the lens of the Persian legend: where was the 'dead dog' — and where were its 'beautiful teeth'? What did everyone else turn away from that deserved a second look?"

3. "Tonight, hold this question: did I see the world today through my own eyes, or through the eyes of my prejudices? Where did true perception break through?"

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "In the fourth month, as a new exercise, what is sometimes called a 'positive attitude' to life should be cultivated. It consists in seeking always for the good, the praiseworthy, the beautiful and the like, in all beings, all experiences, all things. This quality of soul is best characterized by a Persian legend concerning Christ Jesus."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

2. text: "He who consciously turns his mind, for one month, to the positive aspect of all his experiences will gradually notice a feeling creeping into him as if his skin were becoming porous on all sides, and as if his soul were opening wide to all kinds of secret and delicate processes in his environment which hitherto entirely escaped his notice."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

3. text: "There is, however, a difference between a judgment which, proceeding merely from one's own personality, is coloured with the element of personal sympathy or antipathy, and an attitude which enters lovingly into the alien phenomenon or being, always asking: How has it come to be like this or to act like this? Such an attitude will by its very nature be more set upon helping what is imperfect than upon simply finding fault and criticizing."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

4. text: "The student suppresses all superfluous criticism of everything that is imperfect, evil and bad, and seeks rather to understand everything that comes under his notice. Should some trouble befall him he does not proceed to condemn and criticize, but accepts the inevitable, and endeavors to the best of his ability to give the matter a turn for the best."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V"

# FRIDAY (dayIndex 5) — OPENNESS / IMPARTIALITY
## Unbiased Observation | ♀ Venus

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "The esoteric pupil must break entirely with the attitude which, in the face of something just heard or seen, exclaims: 'I never heard that, or I never saw that, before; I don't believe it — it's an illusion.'"
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "At every moment he must be ready to encounter and accept absolutely new experiences. What he has hitherto recognized as being in accordance with natural law should present no obstacle to the acceptance of a new truth."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

3. "Upon hearing some information, the student never says to himself: 'I don't believe it; it contradicts my present opinions.' He is far rather ready to test and rectify his views and opinions."
   — Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V

4. "From every breath of air, from every leaf, from the babbling of children one can learn something if one is prepared to bring to one's aid a certain point of view that one has not made use of up to the present."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Today, did you encounter anything that contradicted what you already believed? What happened inside you at that moment — did you open, or close?"

2. "Think of the last opinion you formed today. Was it genuinely new — or a repetition of something you decided long ago? Can you tell the difference?"

3. "Has anything surprised you today? If not — consider whether the world has been predictable, or whether your perception has been filtering out the new."

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "In your backward review: find one moment where you dismissed something — a remark, an impression, a feeling — before fully receiving it. What would have happened if you had remained open five seconds longer?"

2. "Review one encounter from today. Replay it without your assumptions. What does the other person become when you remove the label you placed on them?"

3. "Tonight, ask: what did I refuse to see today because it did not fit what I already knew? The answer may not come immediately. Wait for it."

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "In the fifth month, efforts should be made to develop the feeling of confronting every new experience with complete open-mindedness. The esoteric pupil must break entirely with the attitude which, in the face of something just heard or seen, exclaims: 'I never heard that, or I never saw that, before; I don't believe it — it's an illusion.' At every moment he must be ready to encounter and accept absolutely new experiences."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

2. text: "Thought linked with will undergoes a certain maturing if we permit ourselves never to be robbed by previous experiences of the unbiased receptivity for new experiences. For the student of the spiritual the following thought should entirely lose its meaning: 'I have never heard that, I do not believe that.'"
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

3. text: "He ever remains receptive for everything that confronts him, and he trusts in the efficacy of his undertakings. Timidity and skepticism are banished from his being. He harbors a faith in the power of his intentions. A hundred failures cannot rob him of this faith. This is the 'faith which can move mountains.'"
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter V"

4. text: "One should not in any way disregard, at any particular stage of life, one's previous experiences. One should judge what one experiences in the present by one's experiences of the past. This is placed upon one scale of the balance; upon the other, however, must be placed the inclination of the student continually to experience the new."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V"

# SATURDAY (dayIndex 6) — HARMONY
## Integration | ♄ Saturn

### morningSeed (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. "In the sixth month, endeavours should be made to repeat all the five exercises again, systematically and in regular alternation. In this way a beautiful equilibrium of soul will gradually develop."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

2. "Anyone who has employed certain consecutive periods of time for the purpose of acquiring these capacities will still be subject to the necessity of bringing them into harmonious concord in his soul."
   — Rudolf Steiner, An Outline of Esoteric Science, GA 13, Chapter V

3. "A mood reconciling all experiences takes possession of the soul, a mood that is by no means one of indifference but, on the contrary, enables one for the first time to work in the world for its genuine progress and improvement."
   — Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245

4. "The training of thoughts and feelings introduces into the soul and spirit the same organic symmetry with which nature has constructed the physical body. Gradually the student learns to breathe and see with the soul, to speak and hear with the spirit."
   — Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter III

### middayCheckpoint (cycle by date % 3)

1. "Which of the five exercises have you practiced most naturally this week — and which have you neglected? The neglected one is where your next growth lies."

2. "Right now: is your thinking clear? Is your will your own? Are your feelings steady? Is your perception open? Is your judgment positive? Which is weakest in this moment?"

3. "Where this week did one exercise come alive — not as a task but as a genuine soul experience? And where did the exercise remain only a word?"

### eveningDeepening (cycle by date % 3)

1. "In your backward review of the week: name one place you drifted, one place you held steady, and one clear intention to carry into the next week."

2. "Review the six days behind you. Where was thought sovereign? Where was will awake? Where was equanimity present? Where did positivity see through the veil? Where did openness receive the new? Let each day speak its own lesson."

3. "Tonight, bring the five exercises together as one gesture. Not five tasks — one life. Where are they already harmonizing in you? Where do they still stand apart?"

### studyFragment (cycle by weekNumber % 4)

1. text: "In the sixth month, endeavours should be made to repeat all the five exercises again, systematically and in regular alternation. In this way a beautiful equilibrium of soul will gradually develop. It will be noticed, especially, that previous dissatisfactions with certain phenomena and beings in the world completely disappear."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

2. text: "The six exercises described paralyse the harmful influence other occult exercises can have, so that only what is beneficial remains. Secondly, these exercises alone ensure that efforts in meditation and concentration will have a positive result."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

3. text: "The very movements and gestures of a person change under the influence of such exercises, and if, one day, he can actually observe that the character of his handwriting has altered, then he may say to himself that he is just about to reach a first rung on the upward path."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, Guidance in Esoteric Training, GA 245, General Demands"

4. text: "The training of thoughts and feelings, pursued in the way described, introduces into the soul and spirit the same organic symmetry with which nature has constructed the physical body. Before this development, soul and spirit are undifferentiated masses. After this training they begin to assume a brilliant yellowish-green, or greenish-blue color, and show a regular structure."
   source: "Rudolf Steiner, How to Know Higher Worlds, GA 10, Chapter III"
